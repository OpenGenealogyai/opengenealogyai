# OpenGenealogyAI — 20 Big Goals (Version C: Platform Play)
*Approved by Garlon Maxwell — 2026-05-07*
*Steward: Garlon Maxwell | Reviewed daily by Big Goal Skill agent*

---

## The Mission in One Sentence
Build the open genealogical standard that lets AI bring family history to every person on earth — fund it by charging fairly for AI research time, pay the people doing the work, and let anyone in the world build on top of it.

---

## The 20 Big Goals

### 1. Establish MaxGen as the Open Genealogy Standard
The MaxGen schema (Maxwell Genealogy Standard) becomes the JSON equivalent of what GEDCOM tried to be — the format every AI tool, genealogy app, and digitization project can build on. CC0 public domain. Garlon Maxwell as steward. Anyone can use it. Nobody can own it.

### 2. Deliver a Free Ancestor Report to Anyone Who Asks
This is the core promise, the marketing engine, and the database seed all in one. No credit card. No account required. Fill out a short form, receive a research report by email within 24 hours. Later, access it in a browser. This is how we earn trust before we earn money.

### 3. Build a Self-Funding Contributor Workforce
Contributors earn 70% of every Research Action dollar their work generates. Quality checkers earn 10%. The platform keeps 20% for coordination and hosting. The more users pay, the more contributors earn. The workforce scales itself — we don't hire anyone.

### 4. Charge for AI Research Time in Plain, Predictable Units
Every time our AI takes one action on your behalf — opening a historical record, comparing two names, searching an archive, checking a date against three sources, scoring whether two people might be the same person — that is one Research Action. Casual users pay $0.12 per action with no subscription. Regular researchers buy monthly bundles: 100 actions for $10, 350 for $29, 1,250 for $100. The AI works through your family tree one action at a time, and you only pay for what it actually does. This is the platform's primary revenue engine and the pool that pays contributors.

### 5. Build Document Storage as a Separate Paid Product
Photos, certificates, letters, audio recordings, home videos — stored securely, linked directly to the family tree. Flat-rate tiers: $2/month for up to 1,000 documents, $5/month for up to 5,000 documents and photos, $12/month for up to 20,000 items including video and audio. 100% platform revenue — contributors are not paid from storage fees. Users own their files and can download them any time.

### 6. Route 100% of Donations Directly to Active Contributors
Donations are pooled and distributed monthly, weighted by each contributor's verified output that month. If you contributed 12% of all embedded records in a given month, you receive 12% of every dollar donated that month. The platform keeps nothing from donations. Every amount, every distribution, every payout is published in an open ledger on GitHub. $1 donated = $1 distributed.

### 7. Require Contributor Attribution in Every Record, Every Display, Every Tool
Every MaxGen record carries a contributor object — the name of who embedded it and who verified it. Every MaxGen Person carries a contributors array — every person who contributed any source record linked to that individual. Any tool, viewer, or app that displays MaxGen data is required by the standard to show a contributor dropdown. This is not optional. Attribution is permanent and cannot be removed.

### 8. Enable Third-Party Tools on MaxGen Without Permission or Fees
The standard grows when others build on it. Any developer, institution, company, or hobbyist can build a viewer, a research tool, a mobile app, or a database using MaxGen — no license needed, no fee, no approval required. Every tool built on MaxGen grows the database, grows the contributor network, and grows our user base. We set the standard. Others help build the world.

### 9. Launch OpenGenealogyAI.com and OpenGenealogyAI.org
Simple, fast, to the point. The free ancestor report is above the fold. Pricing is clear. No noise. Both domains point to the same platform. These are respected websites that genealogists, developers, institutions, and donors find credible and useful. First impression = trust.

### 10. Use GitHub as the Canonical Repository
The MaxGen standard, all schemas, the public database, governance rules, contributor ledger, audit logs, and open-source pipeline code all live on GitHub. Public, permanent, forkable. GitHub is not just where the code lives — it is where the standard is proven to be real and open.

### 11. Accept Display Advertising as a Revenue Stream
OpenGenealogyAI is a quota-free organization — display advertising is appropriate and expected. Ads run on public-facing pages. Revenue supplements Research Action income without changing the product or mission. Genealogy is a high-intent audience and commands strong CPM rates.

### 12. Set Up Payment Processing Before the First Paid User
Apple Pay, PayPal, and credit card acceptance must be live before any paying customer arrives. No friction between intent and purchase. Receipts emailed immediately. Recurring subscriptions managed automatically. Payouts to contributors via PayPal or ACH.

### 13. Build a CRM to Track Every User from Day One
Every person who requests a free report, signs up for storage, buys a Research Action bundle, or joins the contributor waitlist is a contact in our CRM. We know who they are, what they need, when to follow up, and how they heard about us. No customer falls through the cracks.

### 14. Deploy the Multi-Agent Operating System
Five agents with defined roles, rules, and feedback loops:
- **Marketing Agent** — drives traffic, manages content, measures acquisition
- **Sales Agent** — converts free users to paid, manages upgrade prompts
- **Social Media Agent** — posts, engages, builds following across platforms
- **Quality Control Agent** — reviews platform operations, flags anomalies
- **Data Quality Agent** — enforces schema compliance, runs validation pipeline, quarantines bad records

Each agent has a written specification, measurable goals, and a feedback loop that improves its performance over time.

### 15. Run a Daily AI Goal Review — Slack Message Every Morning
Every morning, Garlon receives a Slack message from the Big Goal Skill agent. It contains:
- A progress summary across all 20 goals
- Open questions requiring a decision (each presented as 3 choices with a recommendation highlighted)
- What Grok and ChatGPT would change about the current goals or approach
- Any agent issues flagged overnight
Garlon answers in Slack. The agent incorporates the answers, updates goals, and creates tasks.

### 16. Publish a Governance Model for Schema Changes
Anyone can propose a MaxGen schema change via a GitHub Issue using a standard template. The community discusses it openly. Garlon Maxwell has final approval — nothing merges without his sign-off. Changes are versioned (v1.0 → v1.1 for additions, v1.0 → v2.0 for breaking changes). Records written to any version remain readable forever. No silent migration. No fragmentation.

### 17. Reach 1 Million Embedded Records
One million records in the Qdrant vector database is the threshold where AI semantic search becomes genuinely impressive — where "find my German ancestors in Kentucky around 1820" returns real, useful results. The pipeline is currently running at 139,000 embedded records and growing. This goal is in reach.

### 18. Build the Community Trees Directory
A global registry where anyone can list their own genealogy collection — hosted anywhere, in any format. MaxGen-format trees get a verified badge and are indexed by our AI. GEDCOM and other formats get a free conversion offer. The directory is an SEO asset, a goodwill generator, and a pipeline for new records. It embodies the spirit of global genealogy collaboration.

### 19. Build the Big Goal Skill — a Self-Improving Daily Review Loop
The Big Goal Skill is the system that keeps everything on track. It reads the goals, reads the agents, reads the task specs, checks the pipeline, and produces a daily report. It proposes changes to goals and tasks based on what it observes. It runs multi-AI brainstorming (Grok + ChatGPT + Claude) on hard questions. It tracks its own recommendations to see which ones Garlon accepted, so it can improve over time. The system gets smarter every day.

### 20. Build a Brand Worth Following
OpenGenealogyAI is recognized and respected by genealogists, developers, institutions, and donors. The MaxGen name means something. The contributor model is talked about as a new way to fund open data. The free ancestor report is what people tell their family about. People refer others without being asked. The brand grows the mission, and the mission grows the brand.

## How Goals Become Tasks
Each goal is broken into tasks by the Big Goal Skill agent. Tasks are tracked in `.flow/`. No task is started without a spec. No spec is written without a goal to reference. The chain is: Mission → Goals → Tasks → Agent Actions → Results → Goal Review.

## Review Cadence
- **Daily**: Slack message from Big Goal Skill agent with progress + questions + recommendations
- **Weekly**: Goal health check — are we moving? What's blocked?
- **Monthly**: Full goal review — do any goals need to change? Are the agents performing?

### 21. Build the Paid User Portal
When someone pays for Research Actions or document storage, they get more than a service — they get a home for their family history. The paid portal is a private, secure dashboard where users can log in and see everything we've built for them.

The portal has six components:

**User Accounts** — Email + password login, or Google/Apple sign-in. A free-report user who upgrades gets their existing data automatically pulled into their account. No re-entry.

**Private Dashboard** — After login, the user lands on their tree overview: how many generations found, how many documents attached, last updated date, and one-click access to all views.

**Interactive Pedigree Chart** — A clickable, zoomable ancestor tree built with D3.js. Each person is a card showing name, birth year, and death year. Confidence scores display as color intensity — deep gold for high confidence, faded for uncertain or inferred. Clicking any person opens their detail panel.

**Family Group Sheets** — Auto-generated from the person's data: one sheet per family unit (parents + children), with all dates, places, and source citations listed. Printable. Downloadable as PDF.

**Document Library** — Every photo, certificate, letter, or recording the user uploads is stored here, linked to specific people in the tree. Organized by person and document type. This is Goal #5 made real and visible.

**Tree Sharing** — Generate a private shareable link for family members. Viewers can browse but not edit. Invite specific people as collaborators.

This is the product paying customers are actually buying. Everything else — free reports, Research Actions, document storage, contributor attribution — feeds into this portal as the destination where it all comes together.
