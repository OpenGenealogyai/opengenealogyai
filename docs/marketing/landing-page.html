Genealogy Has a Truth Problem

Every major genealogy platform forces a single answer. When two records disagree, one gets deleted. When researchers fight over who someone's mother was, the loudest voice wins. The uncertainty — the most honest part of historical research — disappears.

Ancestry / FamilySearch

One answer per fact. Edit wars. Proprietary data you can't export. Your research locked in their system.

GEDCOM Standard

40-year-old file format. No confidence scores. No source provenance. No machine readability for AI agents.

OpenGenealogyAI

Every fact carries a confidence score and its source. Multiple possible parents coexist. Open JSON standard. Agent-verifiable.

How Confidence Scores Work

Instead of a single answer, we give every assertion a score from 0.0 to 1.0 based on documentary evidence.

Who was Abraham Lincoln's father? (b. 1809)
Thomas Lincoln
0.82 — birth record + census + 3 witnesses
Abraham Enloe
0.12 — 1899 claim, single source, disputed
Both assertions preserved. No one's research is deleted. A researcher can cite either.

The Open Standard

OpenGenealogyAI is a JSON schema standard, not just a website. Any researcher, application, or AI agent can produce or consume our records.

RawRecord

Source documents exactly as found — birth certificates, census rows, parish registers — with provenance and confidence.

Person

Probabilistic identity with multiple possible parents, confidence-scored name assertions, and an immutable assertion log.

TaskQueue

Distributed work queue for AI agents — ingest, judge, build, resolve conflicts — with cost caps and human gates built in.
