## Template B — University Libraries / Special Collections

**Subject:** Open-Source AI Genealogy Indexing — Partnership Inquiry

Dear [Librarian/Archivist Name],

I'm writing about [Institution]'s [specific collection name] — we believe it contains genealogical records that would significantly benefit from structured AI indexing.

Our project, OpenGenealogyAI, converts historical documents into a probabilistic genealogy standard: instead of forcing a single "answer" about who someone's parents were, we produce confidence-scored assertions with full source citation. Every extracted fact links back to the source document URL.

**For your institution this means:**
- The collection gets machine-readable metadata (our RawRecord schema)
- Full credit preserved in every extracted record
- All data CC0 — no proprietary claims on your archives
- A public-facing searchable index at opengenealogyai.org at launch

We're particularly interested in collections covering [relevant region/period based on the institution]. Does [Institution] have any collections that meet the following criteria?
- Pre-1920 records (well outside living-person window)
- Contains names, dates, locations, and relationships
- Currently available under CC0, public domain, or similar open license

I'd welcome a 20-minute call to discuss what would be involved. Our full technical documentation is at github.com/opengenealogyai.

Warm regards,

We're looking for experienced genealogical researchers to:

2. Help us build test fixtures for cases where parentage is genuinely disputed in the historical record

The project is fully open source. Contributors are credited in the CONTRIBUTORS file and in the metadata of every record they help validate.
