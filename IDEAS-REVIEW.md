# OpenGenealogyAI — 10 Ideas to Review
*Generated 2026-05-06 — review these tomorrow*

---

## 1. Surname Cluster Hubs
**What it is:** AI groups all MaxGen records for a given surname in a region + time period into a shared collaborative workspace. "Maxwell — Kentucky 1780–1860" becomes one room where everyone researching that branch works together. Records contributed by anyone get surfaced to everyone in that cluster.

**Revenue angle:** Premium cluster memberships ($5/mo) for active collaboration. Free to read, paid to contribute notes, flags, or merge suggestions.

**Why it matters:** Brick walls fall faster when 40 people are working the same problem. No other platform does this automatically by surname + region.

---

## 2. Cemetery Mapping App
**What it is:** A mobile-friendly web app (or PWA) for photographing headstones in the field. GPS-tags the location, runs OCR on the photo, extracts name + dates, and creates a draft MaxGen Record. Contributors walk local cemeteries on a Sunday afternoon and submit 50 new records.

**Revenue angle:** Contributor earnings (CPU task, no GPU needed). Cemeteries are a huge untapped source — millions of headstones never digitized.

**Why it matters:** Great for people without a GPU who still want to contribute. Extremely viral ("I digitized my hometown cemetery today").

---

## 3. Obituary Pipeline
**What it is:** Continuously pull and parse modern obituaries from funeral home websites, newspapers, and Legacy.com (with appropriate licensing). Each obituary is a goldmine — full name, birth/death dates, spouse, children, parents, hometown. AI converts to MaxGen Person records automatically.

**Revenue angle:** Subscriber-only access to recent obituary data (last 5 years). Older data is free. Serious researchers pay $29+/mo for this alone.

**Why it matters:** Most genealogy databases stop at 1950. This extends the database to 2026 with current-generation data, which is what people actually need to connect to living family.

---

## 4. The Brick Wall Network
**What it is:** A structured forum where researchers post their unsolvable genealogy problem ("Who were Richard Maxwell's parents? b. ~1796 KY, d. ~1821"). Other researchers can take on the case manually. AI agents can be assigned to work the problem autonomously. Optionally post a bounty ($10–$500) for a verified solution.

**Revenue angle:** Bounty platform fee (10% of bounty). AI agent research moves billed normally. Monthly subscription for "premium cases" with faster AI turnaround.

**Why it matters:** Every genealogist hits a brick wall. This turns the community's collective knowledge into a searchable problem-solving engine. The bounty system is novel and press-worthy.

---

## 5. Heritage Print Book
**What it is:** One-click print-on-demand family history book auto-generated from a MaxGen tree. AI writes the narrative chapter by chapter ("The Story of the Maxwell Family in Kentucky"). User reviews and edits. We print and ship a hardcover book. Beautiful coffee-table format.

**Revenue angle:** $49 softcover, $79 hardcover, $129 leather. High margins. Zero research compute cost — it's pure formatting + print-on-demand.

**Why it matters:** Extremely giftable product. Every family reunion is a sales opportunity. Grandmothers will buy these. Extremely low cost to build (use a print-on-demand API like Lulu or Blurb).

---

## 6. Family Migration Map
**What it is:** A beautiful animated map showing your family's geographic movement across generations — from Ireland to New York to Kentucky to Utah. Colors by generation. Exportable as a sharable image, GIF, or short video.

**Revenue angle:** Free basic map (watermarked). $9 one-time for high-res export. $5/mo to keep it live and updating as new records are added.

**Why it matters:** The most shareable genealogy product imaginable. Goes viral on social media. "My family's 400-year journey across 3 continents" — people post these. It's a marketing machine.

---

## 7. Raw DNA File Bridge
**What it is:** Upload your raw DNA file from 23andMe, AncestryDNA, or MyHeritage. Our AI cross-references it against the MaxGen database to suggest matches and fill in tree branches. No DNA hosting — we process and discard the raw file, store only the match results.

**Revenue angle:** $29 one-time DNA analysis (fits Pro plan or standalone). Recurring value if the database grows.

**Why it matters:** DNA + documentary genealogy together crack cases neither can solve alone. This is the killer feature the big platforms charge $30/month for. We can offer it cheaper as a one-time shot.

---

## 8. Adoption & Donor-Conceived Registry
**What it is:** A sensitive, private sub-platform for adoptees, donor-conceived individuals, and others searching for biological relatives. Uses DNA + documentary genealogy. Private by default — you opt in to being found. AI suggests probable connections.

**Revenue angle:** $15/month standalone subscription (audience is highly motivated to pay). Separate brand identity possible (doesn't have to be OpenGenealogyAI — more clinical/neutral).

**Why it matters:** Massive underserved market. 140,000+ US adoptions per year. Donor-conceived community is exploding. High emotional value = willingness to pay. Could be its own company.

---

## 9. Historical Newspaper Name Index
**What it is:** License or scrape newspaper archives (Chronicling America is free/public domain), OCR every page, index every name mention. When a user researches an ancestor, we surface newspaper hits automatically — birth announcements, obituaries, court records, land sales, church news.

**Revenue angle:** Included in Pro/Power plans. Massive competitive advantage over platforms that don't have this.

**Why it matters:** FamilySearch and Ancestry partially do this but their indexes are incomplete and siloed. An open, AI-built index that contributors can extend is a genuine differentiator. Chronicling America alone has 20M+ pages.

---

## 10. Living Memory Voice Archive
**What it is:** Record yourself or an elderly relative telling family stories on any device. AI transcribes and timestamps the audio, links names mentioned to MaxGen Person records, and creates a searchable voice archive. "Hear your great-grandmother describe Sunday dinners in 1942."

**Revenue angle:** Storage-based ($2/mo + per-MB). Extremely high emotional value. Near-zero compute cost after initial transcription.

**Why it matters:** The most irreplaceable product we could build. Once someone is gone, the voice is gone. This creates urgency. Feature that no genealogy platform takes seriously. Also creates a massive training dataset of family oral histories for future AI models.
