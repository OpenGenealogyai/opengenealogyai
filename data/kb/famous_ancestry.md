# Famous Ancestry — Known and Verified Facts

This file contains documented ancestry information about well-known historical and public figures. All information is drawn from established genealogical research, peer-reviewed scholarship, and reputable genealogical databases. Ada should cite uncertainty where it exists and direct users to primary sources when possible.

## Abraham Lincoln (1809–1865)
16th President of the United States

**Born:** February 12, 1809, Hardin County (now LaRue County), Kentucky
**Died:** April 15, 1865, Washington, D.C. (assassinated)

**Parents:**
- Father: Thomas Lincoln (January 6, 1778 – January 17, 1851), born in Rockingham County, Virginia; a farmer and carpenter
- Mother: Nancy Hanks Lincoln (c. February 5, 1784 – October 5, 1818), born in Hampshire County, Virginia (now West Virginia); died of milk sickness in Indiana

**Grandparents (paternal):**
- Grandfather: Captain Abraham Lincoln Sr. (c. 1744 – May 1786), born in Virginia; killed by a Native American raid in Jefferson County, Kentucky, in 1786 — the event that shaped Thomas Lincoln's childhood
- Grandmother: Bathsheba Herring Lincoln (c. 1742 – 1836)

**Great-grandparents (paternal):**
- John Lincoln (c. 1716 – c. 1788), great-grandfather; born in Virginia; emigrated from Berks County, Pennsylvania, to Virginia's Shenandoah Valley (Augusta County) around 1768
- Rebecca Flowers Lincoln

**Colonial line:**
The Lincoln family in America traces back to **Samuel Lincoln** (c. 1622 – 1690), who emigrated from Hingham, Norfolk, England, to Hingham, Massachusetts, in 1637. Samuel Lincoln is Abraham Lincoln's great-great-great-great-grandfather (6 generations back). This line is one of the best-documented in American presidential genealogy.

The line runs: Samuel Lincoln → Mordecai Lincoln → Mordecai Lincoln II → John Lincoln → Abraham Lincoln Sr. → Thomas Lincoln → Abraham Lincoln.

**Other ancestry notes:**
- Nancy Hanks's ancestry has been more contested. She was likely the daughter of James Hanks and Lucy Shipley, though questions remain about the exact family configuration.
- President Lincoln was aware of his frontier Kentucky origins and the death of his grandfather Abraham, which he wrote about in correspondence.

## George Washington (1732–1799)
1st President of the United States

**Born:** February 22, 1732 (New Style), Pope's Creek, Westmoreland County, Virginia
**Died:** December 14, 1799, Mount Vernon, Virginia

**Parents:**
- Father: Augustine Washington (April 12, 1694 – April 12, 1743), planter and iron mine operator
- Mother: Mary Ball Washington (1708 – August 25, 1789)

**Grandparents (paternal):**
- Grandfather: Lawrence Washington (c. 1659 – 1698)
- Grandmother: Mildred Warner Washington (c. 1671 – 1701)

**Colonial line — John Washington:**
The Washington family in America traces back to **John Washington** (c. 1632 – 1677), George Washington's great-grandfather, who emigrated from Northamptonshire, England, to Virginia in 1657 or 1658. John Washington settled in Westmoreland County and acquired extensive land holdings. He was a burgess (member of the Virginia House of Burgesses) and colonel of the militia.

The line runs: John Washington → Lawrence Washington → Augustine Washington → George Washington.

**English origins:**
The Washington family originated from **Sulgrave, Northamptonshire, England**. The manor of Sulgrave was purchased by Lawrence Washington (George's 6th great-grandfather) in 1539. Sulgrave Manor is now a museum dedicated to the Washington family's English origins. The family coat of arms — two horizontal red bars and three red stars on a white field — is believed to be the inspiration for the design of the American flag.

Further back, the Washington family traces to William de Hertburn, who took the name "de Wessington" (from the village of Wessington in County Durham) in the 13th century.

## British Royal Family

### Queen Elizabeth II (1926–2022)
**Born:** April 21, 1926, Mayfair, London
**Died:** September 8, 2022, Balmoral Castle, Scotland

**Parents:**
- Father: King George VI (1895–1952)
- Mother: Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother, née Elizabeth Bowes-Lyon (1900–2002)

**Descent from William the Conqueror:**
Queen Elizabeth II descended from William I, known as William the Conqueror (c. 1028 – 1087), who conquered England at the Battle of Hastings in 1066. The descent is well-documented through approximately 30 generations, primarily through the Plantagenet kings. This is a verified genealogical line accepted by the College of Arms and documented in Burke's Peerage.

The British royal family has maintained documented genealogical records stretching back further than almost any other family on earth, in part because succession to the throne made accurate records a matter of state necessity.

### King Charles III (born 1948)
**Born:** November 14, 1948, Buckingham Palace, London

**Parents:**
- Mother: Queen Elizabeth II (1926–2022)
- Father: Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh (1921–2021), of the House of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg

**Prince Philip's line:**
Prince Philip was born in Corfu, Greece, as a Prince of Greece and Denmark. His father was Prince Andrew of Greece and Denmark; his mother was Princess Alice of Battenberg (a great-granddaughter of Queen Victoria). Philip descended from King George I of Greece (originally Prince Wilhelm of Denmark) and from the German Houses of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg and Battenberg/Mountbatten.

**Extended royal connections:**
Through Queen Victoria (1819–1901), who was grandmother to many European monarchs, the British Royal Family is related by descent or marriage to virtually every royal house in Europe. Victoria had nine children who married into the royal families of Germany, Russia, Greece, Romania, Sweden, and Spain — earning her the nickname "the grandmother of Europe."

**Medieval descent:**
The documented royal line extends back 40+ generations. Key ancestors include:
- Henry II of England (1133–1189) and Eleanor of Aquitaine (1122–1204)
- Edward I of England (1239–1307), "Longshanks"
- Henry V of England (1386–1422), victor of Agincourt
- Margaret Beaufort (1443–1509), mother of Henry VII and matriarch of the Tudor dynasty
- James VI of Scotland / James I of England (1566–1625), who united the crowns

## Barack Obama (born 1961)
44th President of the United States

**Born:** August 4, 1961, Honolulu, Hawaii

**Parents:**
- Father: Barack Obama Sr. (1936–1982), from Kogelo, Kenya; of the Luo people
- Mother: Ann Dunham (1942–1995), from Wichita, Kansas

**Maternal line (Dunham family):**
Ann Dunham's family has been traced by genealogists at Ancestry.com and by the New England Historic Genealogical Society. Her ancestry traces back to early American colonial settlers, primarily of English origin, in Maryland, Virginia, and the Carolinas.

**Notable genealogical connections:**
- Barack Obama is documented as a **9th cousin** of President George W. Bush, through a common ancestor: Samuel Hinckley (c. 1589–1662), who settled in Barnstable, Massachusetts. This connection was identified by the New England Historic Genealogical Society in 2007.
- Obama has a documented distant cousin relationship with **Dick Cheney** — approximately 8th cousins — through Mareen Duvall, a French Huguenot who emigrated to Maryland in the 1650s. This connection was publicly announced by Cheney's wife Lynne Cheney.
- Obama's ancestry reportedly includes documented descent from **John Punch**, believed to be the first African in colonial Virginia to be legally enslaved for life (1640). This connection, identified by Ancestry.com researchers in 2012, makes Obama the first known US president with documented African-American ancestry on his maternal (white) side.

**African paternal line:**
Barack Obama Sr.'s Kenyan Luo ancestry is genealogically distinct. Oral genealogical traditions among the Luo trace family lines but are not integrated into Western genealogical databases.

## Benjamin Franklin (1706–1790)
Founding Father, statesman, inventor, printer

**Born:** January 17, 1706, Boston, Massachusetts
**Died:** April 17, 1790, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

**Parents:**
- Father: Josiah Franklin (1657–1745), a tallow chandler; born in Ecton, Northamptonshire, England; emigrated to Boston around 1683
- Mother: Abiah Folger Franklin (1667–1752), born in Nantucket, Massachusetts

**English origins:**
Josiah Franklin was born in Ecton, Northamptonshire — the same county from which the Washington family originated. Genealogical research in Ecton parish registers documents the Franklin family there from the early 1500s. Benjamin Franklin visited Ecton in 1758 and 1771, where he met relatives and consulted the parish registers himself — making him one of the earliest examples of an American returning to research his English roots.

**Maternal line (Folger family):**
Abiah Folger was descended from Peter Folger (1617–1690), one of the early settlers of Nantucket. Peter Folger was an early advocate of religious tolerance and wrote verse defending Baptist and Quaker dissenters.

## Winston Churchill (1874–1965)
British Prime Minister, 1940–1945 and 1951–1955

**Born:** November 30, 1874, Blenheim Palace, Oxfordshire, England
**Died:** January 24, 1965, London

**Parents:**
- Father: Lord Randolph Churchill (1849–1895), younger son of the 7th Duke of Marlborough
- Mother: Jennie Jerome Churchill (1854–1921), born in Brooklyn, New York — an American

**Spencer-Churchill line:**
Winston Churchill's paternal line descends from **John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough** (1650–1722), the great general who defeated Louis XIV's forces at Blenheim (1704) and Ramillies (1706). The family seat, Blenheim Palace, was built in his honor. Churchill's family belongs to the Spencer-Churchill line descending from the 5th Duke of Marlborough.

**American connection:**
Through his mother Jennie Jerome, Churchill was half-American. His maternal grandfather Leonard Jerome (1817–1891) was a prominent New York financier and horse-racing enthusiast. Churchill often invoked his American heritage in his wartime speeches to strengthen the Anglo-American alliance.

**American presidential connection:**
Winston Churchill and Franklin D. Roosevelt were documented distant cousins — both descended from Henry Howland (c. 1603–1671), a Pilgrim who came to Plymouth Colony in 1621. This makes them approximately 6th cousins, twice removed.

## Cleopatra VII (69 BC – 30 BC)
Last Pharaoh of Ancient Egypt

**Born:** c. 69 BC, Alexandria, Egypt
**Died:** August 12, 30 BC, Alexandria (suicide)

**Parents:**
- Father: Ptolemy XII Auletes (c. 117 BC – 51 BC)
- Mother: Unknown (likely a concubine; possibly Cleopatra V Tryphaena, though this is debated)

**Dynasty:**
Cleopatra VII belonged to the **Ptolemaic dynasty**, a Macedonian Greek royal house founded by **Ptolemy I Soter** (367 BC – 283 BC), one of Alexander the Great's generals, who became Pharaoh of Egypt after Alexander's death in 323 BC. Despite ruling Egypt for over 300 years, the Ptolemaic rulers were ethnically Macedonian Greek, not Egyptian. Cleopatra was reportedly the first Ptolemaic ruler to bother learning the Egyptian language.

**Extended line:**
The Ptolemaic dynasty practiced extensive sibling and cousin marriages to consolidate power, making genealogical lines within the family extremely complex. Cleopatra's great-great-grandmother was Berenice III, whose line traced back to Ptolemy I.

## Princess Diana (1961–1997)
Diana, Princess of Wales

**Born:** July 1, 1961, Sandringham, Norfolk, England
**Died:** August 31, 1997, Paris, France

**Parents:**
- Father: John Spencer, 8th Earl Spencer (1924–1992)
- Mother: Frances Roche Shand Kydd (1936–2004)

**Spencer line:**
The Spencer family is one of England's oldest noble families, with documented ancestry going back to the 15th century. Diana's ancestors include the 1st Duke of Marlborough through a Spencer collateral line.

**Royal connections through non-royal lines:**
Genealogists have noted that Diana Spencer actually had more documented royal descent than her husband Prince Charles through the older English aristocracy. Through her Spencer and Cavendish ancestors, she descended from King Charles II (1630–1685) of England through his illegitimate children — a descent shared by many English aristocratic families.

**Her children's ancestry:**
Prince William (born 1982, now King William V) and Prince Harry (born 1984) combine the documented royal lines of the House of Windsor with the Spencer aristocratic line, giving them descent from both sides of the English peerage.

## Research Notes for Ada

When users ask about famous ancestry, Ada should:

1. **Distinguish proven connections from family legend.** Many people claim descent from famous figures based on unverified trees. A claimed connection must be supported by a complete, documented chain from the famous person to the user.

2. **Be honest about time depth.** Connections to medieval royalty (Charlemagne, William the Conqueror) are mathematically likely for most people of Western European descent — because 30+ generations ago there were so few people alive that almost everyone of European ancestry is descended from medieval royalty. This is mathematically interesting but genealogically unverified for most individuals.

3. **Guide toward primary sources.** The New England Historic Genealogical Society, Ancestry.com's ProGenealogists, and peer-reviewed genealogical journals (the National Genealogical Society Quarterly, the American Genealogist) publish verified famous-ancestry research.

4. **Never fabricate connections.** If a user asks whether they might be related to Lincoln or Washington, Ada should explain the research process required to establish such a connection — it cannot be assumed or implied.
